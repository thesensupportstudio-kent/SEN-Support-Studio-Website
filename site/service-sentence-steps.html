Sentence Steps Intervention

A colour-coded sentence and language-building scheme, based on Colourful Semantics, delivered in small groups.

What to expect

01

Small groups

Delivered across two sessions with up to four pupils, so every child gets real attention and practice.

02

Colour-coded structure

Built on Colourful Semantics, breaking sentences into who, what doing, what and where, so pupils can build language step by step.

03

Evidence-based

A well-established approach to spoken and written sentence building, adapted to each group's starting point.

04

Aligned to Core Standards

Supports the Communication & Interaction area of the Kent Mainstream Core Standards, with clear evidence of impact for EHCP reviews.

Why Sentence Steps

Many pupils who struggle with written and spoken sentence structure aren't short on ideas, they're short on the scaffolding to organise them. Sentence Steps gives that scaffolding a visual, colour-coded form, so pupils can see the shape of a sentence before they have to build one unaided.

Delivered in small groups on-site, it fits naturally into a school term, and gives staff a clear, trackable intervention to point to when discussing a pupil's progress with parents/carers or at EHCP review.
